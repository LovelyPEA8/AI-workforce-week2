Copilot Chat is the basic AI chat you use to ask questions or get help writing.
It mainly uses internet information and simple inputs you give it, not your company’s internal data.

Use it when: you just need quick answers, ideas, or help with small everyday tasks.

Microsoft 365 Copilot (Copilot for Business) is a more powerful version built into work apps like Word, Excel, Outlook, and Teams.
It can read and use your emails, files, meetings, and calendar to help you do actual work tasks. 
So instead of just answering questions, it can draft reports, summarize meetings, or analyze your documents.

Use it when: you want AI to actively help with your daily job using your real work data.

Copilot Studio is different, it’s not for chatting or doing work directly.
It’s a tool for creating your own custom AI assistants (like a chatbot for your company or customers). 
You can connect it to systems like databases or apps and design how it behaves.

Use it when: you want to build a tailored AI solution (for example, a customer support bot or internal help assistant).

In short:
* Copilot Chat = ask questions and get answers
* Microsoft 365 Copilot = AI that helps you do your job
* Copilot Studio = build your own AI tools
  
The key difference is whether you’re just using AI, using it deeply in your work, or actually creating your own AI assistant.
